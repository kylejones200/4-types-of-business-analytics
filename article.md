### 4 Types of Business Analytics
Business Analytics can be a broad term. Here are the four basic things
you can do with business analytics.


<figcaption>Photo by Isaac Smith on Unsplash</figcaption>


1.  [Descriptive Analysis: Interpret historical data and KPIs to
    identify trends and patterns. This allows you to have a global
    vision of what has happened and what is happening using data
    aggregation and extraction techniques. Many companies use
    descriptive analytics to drill down into customer behavior and how
    to target those customers with marketing strategies.]
2.  [Diagnostic Analysis: Determine what elements influence specific
    trends. This is done by using drilldown, data discovery, data
    extraction, and correlation to reveal the cause of specific events.
    Once an understanding of probability is reached, and why an event
    can occur, algorithms are used for classification and
    regression.]
3.  [Predictive Analytics: Predict and evaluate future results using
    statistical models and Machine Learning techniques. This type of
    analysis typically takes the results of descriptive analytics to
    create models that determine the probability of specific results.
    This type is often used by sales and marketing teams to predict the
    sentiments of specific customers based on data from social
    media.]
4.  [Prescriptive Analysis: Use historic data to recommend how to handle
    similar situations in the future. This type of business analytics
    not only determines the results, but can also recommend the specific
    actions that must occur to have the best possible result. This type
    of business analysis is often used to tailor various options to a
    consumer's real-time needs.]
